CHAPTER ONE

Before You

There was a version of my days that ran on autopilot. Classes, mess food, the same walk back to the hostel. Nothing wrong with any of it. Just... grey at the edges.

I didn't know I was waiting. Then you showed up, and I understood — I had been thinking of you before I even knew your name.

CHAPTER TWO

Love You, Jaanu

You are the kind of person who laughs with your whole body — not just your face — and somehow make everyone around you feel like they were already in on the joke.

You remember things. Quietly. Exactly what pastry I like from Master Bake, the exact moment I need you to just sit with me, and somehow always the right thing to say when I run out of words.

You have this attitude — the kind that doesn't push through hard days so much as it simply refuses to let hard days win. It changes the weather in every room you enter.

Before college ended, before Bangalore, before any of this — there were phone calls that ran way past midnight, BCA Fest & Sports Day memories I still smile at for no reason, and a farewell dance that made me more nervous than any exam. And somewhere in all of it, I looked at you and realised: enna ponnu — ninna matte nanna jaga bere aagide.

My Jaanu,

I wrote this late at night. After everyone was asleep. Because that's the only time it felt quiet enough to say what I actually wanted to say.

There was a version of me before you — fine, ordinary, a little grey around the edges. He didn't know what was missing until you showed up and suddenly everything had a reason. I wasn't waiting. I just hadn't met you yet.

Three years hasn't been a straight line. Some of it was hard in ways I didn't see coming. But you never once made me feel like we weren't going to figure it out. Not once. That's not nothing, Jaanu. That's everything.

I still lose every race to finish the panipuri before you. I don't think boiled rice has ever tasted as good as when we shared it together. I don't think a park bench has ever felt more like home than the one we call ours. These are the things I didn't know I'd love about us — but I love them more than most big things.

Happy 23rd birthday, my Jaanu. And happy three years to us — the most beautiful coincidence of my life. Preethi.
