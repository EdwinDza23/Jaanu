CHAPTER SIX

Campus Days & Bangalore

Before any of this, there were phone calls that ran way past midnight — the kind that always ended with "okay, let's actually sleep now," followed by twenty more minutes of talking anyway.

We bunked more classes than either of us will ever admit to on record. Somehow an hour with you always felt more useful than the lecture we were skipping.

Our farewell dance is still the most nervous I've ever been on a stage — and somehow, still one of my favourite memories of us.

Then Bangalore happened. Real jobs, real rent, real life — and somehow it turned into shopping trips that ran three hours longer than planned, park benches we quietly claimed as "ours," and dates that started as errands and ended up the best part of the week.

My Jaanu,

If you're reading this, it means you made it through every chapter — the quiet ones, the golden ones, and the loud, laughing ones in between.

I keep thinking about the version of me before you. He was fine. Quiet. A little grey around the edges. He didn't know what he was waiting for until you walked into the rest of his life.

Three years hasn't been a straight line, but somehow it's been ours the whole way — from 2 a.m. phone calls in college, to bunked classes we still pretend we regret, to a farewell dance I was more nervous about than any exam. Somewhere between Mangalore beaches, Bangalore shopping trips, and a marathon I never saw coming, staying stopped being a decision. It just became who we are.

I loved you before I had the words for it. I love you now, on your twenty-third birthday, louder than I've ever said it. And I will keep loving you — not because it's easy, but because every ordinary day with you turns out to be the best part of my week.

Happy birthday, my Jaanu. Here's to three years — and to every year we haven't lived yet.

— Yours, always, Edwin
